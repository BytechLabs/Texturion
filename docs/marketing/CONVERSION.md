# Loonext Marketing — Conversion & Clarity Doctrine (BINDING)

User directive: "Don't confuse the customer. MAXIMUM CONVERSION. Interactive. Beautiful."
Binding for all loop iterations, equal authority to VISUALS.md. When richness/honesty/detail
conflict with clarity, CLARITY WINS — simplify, don't add.

The buyer is a busy tradesperson on a phone. If they have to think about what this is or what
to do next, we've lost them. Every page: instantly clear, one obvious next step, beautiful,
and interactive in ways that SELL.

---

## 1. The 5-second test (every page passes)
Above the fold, a first-timer must grasp: **what it is** (a shared text-message inbox for your
business number), **who it's for** (small service businesses), **the one thing to do** (Start
for $29). One headline, one subhead, one primary button. No competing messages.

## 2. One primary CTA, everywhere, low friction
- Primary action is ALWAYS the same words and the same petrol button: **"Start for $29"** →
  /signup. It appears in the nav, the hero, after each major proof section, and in the closing
  band. On mobile it is always reachable (sticky where sensible).
- ONE primary CTA per view. A secondary "See how it works" (scroll/anchor) is allowed but must
  be visually quieter. Never two equally-weighted buttons competing.
- Reduce friction words near CTAs: "Start for $29", "Get your number", "See your inbox" —
  active, concrete, zero jargon. Pair the CTA with a risk-reducer ("Month to month. 30-day
  money-back guarantee.") so clicking feels safe.

## 3. Don't confuse — tame the honest complexity
Loonext's honest-but-complex truths (US 10DLC registration wait, message segments, opt-out
compliance, the first-month fee) must be present but NEVER confusing:
- **Progressive disclosure.** Lead with the simple benefit; put the mechanism behind a "how
  this works" reveal, a tooltip, or a dedicated page — not inline in the hero.
- **Reframe complexity as done-for-you.** Not "you must complete A2P 10DLC brand + campaign
  registration" → "We handle the carrier paperwork. US texting turns on in a few business days;
  Canada is instant." One sentence, benefit-first.
- **Segments:** say "messages"; explain segments only in a tooltip/one line where billing is
  shown. Never make the visitor do telecom math to understand the price.
- **The fee:** state the all-in first-month number plainly and calmly as a trust move, not a
  surprise — but don't lead the page with it. Simple > exhaustive.
- No jargon anywhere in primary copy (no "A2P", "10DLC", "E.164", "webhook", "TCR" in
  headlines/subheads; those belong only on /features/compliance or /security for the curious).

## 4. Message hierarchy: benefit → proof → how → price → act
Every page and the home flow follow the same persuasion spine:
1. **Benefit** (their problem solved, in their words: "Never miss a customer text again").
2. **Proof** (show the real product — screenshots, the live demo — it obviously works).
3. **How** (3 simple steps, visual).
4. **Price** (transparent, framed as the advantage vs per-seat competitors).
5. **Act** (the CTA, with the risk-reducer).
Benefit-first, never feature-first. Customer's problem before our solution.

## 5. Interactivity that converts (not gimmicks)
Every interactive element must move the visitor toward signup by making value tangible:
- **Missed-text calculator** → ends on "That's $X/month walking away. Loonext is $29." → CTA.
- **Crew-size / pricing slider** → visibly proves flat team pricing beats per-seat as the team
  grows → CTA.
- **Live inbox demo** → proves the product is real and simple → "This is your inbox. Start for
  $29."
- **City → area-code widget** → "Your local number is ready in {area code}." → CTA.
Each interactive is beautiful, instantly understandable (no instructions needed), keyboard-
accessible, mobile-friendly, and ends with a nudge to act. If an interaction doesn't help
convince or convert, cut it.

## 6. Trust without fake proof (pre-customer)
No fake logos/testimonials/counts (absolute). Substitute real trust: transparent all-in
pricing on the page, "month-to-month, cancel anytime, 30-day money-back", "we handle carrier
compliance", "your data is never sold", Canada-made, the honest US-timeline shown as candor.
These earn belief without lying.

## 7. Remove anything that confuses or distracts
- No competing CTAs, no dead ends, no walls of options, no unexplained jargon, no cookie
  banner (PostHog cookieless), no chat-widget pop-up, no auto-playing sound, no modal on load.
- Nav is short and scannable; dropdowns group clearly (Product, Trades, Compare). A visitor
  never wonders where to click to buy.
- Cut any section that doesn't advance benefit→proof→how→price→act. Fewer, stronger, clearer.

## 8. Beauty is a conversion feature
Calm, premium, confident, full of the real visual layer (VISUALS.md). A beautiful page is
trusted; a cluttered or empty one is not. Whitespace + one clear path + rich, honest visuals =
conversion. Beauty and clarity are the same goal here, not a tradeoff.

## 9. Conversion QA (every iteration checks these)
- 5-second clarity test on home + one content page (a fresh reader states what/who/next).
- Single obvious primary CTA per view; reachable on mobile; consistent label.
- No jargon in primary copy; complexity is progressively disclosed, never dumped.
- Every interactive ends in a conversion nudge and needs no instructions.
- Path to signup is ≤1 click from any marketing page; no dead ends.
- Nothing on the page competes with or distracts from "Start for $29".
