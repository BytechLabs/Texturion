# Competitor Marketing-Site Teardowns

Researched live 2026-07-02 via direct fetches of simpletexting.com, salesmessage.com (salesmsg.com 301s there), heymarket.com, textline.com, quo.com, podium.com — homepages, pricing pages, sitemaps, and representative industry/comparison pages. Facts below are what the sites showed on that date.

---

## 1. SimpleTexting (simpletexting.com)

### Page structure (homepage, top to bottom)
- **Nav:** Product / Solutions / Resources mega-menus + Pricing + Search + Log In + "Book a Demo" + "Try It Free" + a call/text phone number in the header. Solutions menu is three-axis: by industry (10), by use case (Mass Texting, Group Texting…), by feature (Keywords, Autoresponders…). Resources menu has Education / Support / Tools / Industries sub-columns.
- **Hero:** "A texting service designed to help small businesses win" / "Everyone reads their texts. We help you send them." CTAs: "Start Texting Free" + "Book a Demo", with "No credit card required" underneath. Trust line: "10+ years in business. 17,000+ paying customers. Billions of texts delivered."
- **Social proof:** logo bar (Target, UPS, Chick-fil-A, Starbucks, YMCA, Century 21…), stat-led case-study teasers ("+162% increased coupon redemptions"), 20+ short testimonials, G2 "Best Relationship" badge.
- **Feature sections (order):** everything-you-need overview → grow an audience with opt-in tools → SMS blasts → two-way texting → automated texts → case studies → **compliance section: "SMS marketing is so powerful, it's protected. We help you unlock it"** (carrier approval, built-in compliance tools, human guidance) → 1,000+ integrations → support brag → stats section → "Curious how it works? Send a text to yourself today."
- **Footer:** Learn / Product / Company columns, legal links (Privacy, T&C, **30-day Money-Back Guarantee Policy**, DPA), support hours + phone, Chicago address, iOS/Android badges, socials, and a geo banner: US & Canada only (points international visitors at Sinch Engage).

### Visuals
Real product screenshots framed as example conversations (pottery-artist blast, customer-service exchange, MMS pickup notification), lifestyle collage photos, one promo video, G2 badge. No interactive demo.

### Pricing presentation (/pricing)
- Credit-based with an **interactive volume selector** (500 → 50,000+ texts/mo) and annual/monthly toggle ("Save 20%"). Example: $39/mo yearly for 500 credits.
- Costs stack visibly: **local number $10/mo + $4 one-time carrier registration; extra teammates $20/mo per user beyond 3 seats; extra numbers $10/mo; overage 5.5¢/credit; MMS = 3 credits**.
- Carrier passthrough fees disclosed in FAQ: "approximately $0.0025 per message in the United States and $0.0068 in Canada."
- Rollover credits (one month), 15% nonprofit discount, 30-day refund promise, plan-builder quiz.

### SEO surface
Sitemap index with 18 child sitemaps: comparison pages (9: podium-vs-simpletexting, ez-texting, textedly, textmagic, slicktext, twilio, zingle, plus heymarket-alternative and zipwhip-alternative), **17 industry pages under /industry/** (includes `text-message-marketing-tool-for-home-services` and `beauty-salons-and-spas`; no plumbing/HVAC-specific pages), guides, SMS-compliance hub, SMS-templates-by-industry, success stories, videos, integrations.

The **home-services industry page** anatomy: pain hook (phone tag, 98% open rates) → four use cases (win work, team comms, automation, review prompts) → testimonials → 3-step how-it-works → integrations → three named case studies (Vogel Heating & Cooling, Simon Roofing) → interactive quiz → 7-question FAQ (TCPA, ROI, no-shows). Quote captured: "Texting eliminated about 50% of our phone calls."

### Legal/trust
Privacy, T&C, Money-Back Guarantee policy page, Promo Terms, DPA, System Status, Help Center.

### CTA pattern
Free trial ("Start Texting Free", no CC) + demo booking + live chat/phone with posted hours. Trial length not stated on homepage.

## 2. Salesmsg (salesmsg.com → salesmessage.com)

### Page structure

- **Footer:** Product / Company / Resources columns; legal set includes Terms & Conditions, Terms of Use, Privacy, **Billing Policy**, Acceptable Use Policy, Security & Privacy; app-store badges; status page; 10DLC resource.

### Legal/trust

### CTA pattern
14-day trial with auto-convert + demo + SMS-a-question support line (they text you back — dogfooding).

### Visuals

### Pricing presentation (/pricing)

No HVAC/plumbing pages; home services exists only as a best-practices resource article. **Zero comparison/vs pages.** Free-tools cluster: AI text/email generators (6 of them), SMS templates.

The **salons-and-spas page** anatomy: headline "Text Messaging for Salons, Spas, and Beauty" → appointment confirmations, client Q&A via shared inbox, staff coordination via group messaging, text-enable your existing number → one owner testimonial ("increased appointment bookings from existing clients by over 50%") → Book a Demo. ### Legal/trust

## Cross-site synthesis → implications for Loonext

### Structural conventions everyone follows (safe to adopt)
1. **Homepage skeleton:** nav with Product/Solutions/Pricing → benefit hero + dual CTA → logo/proof bar → 4–7 feature sections alternating copy/screenshot → compliance-trust section → integrations → final CTA. Nobody deviates.
2. **Footers are SEO organs:** 4–10 columns of industry, comparison, resource, and legal links. Plan Loonext's footer as part of the SEO surface from day one.
3. **"No credit card required" sits under every trial CTA** (SimpleTexting, Textline, Heymarket). Loonext is paid-first, so it cannot use this line — the honest substitute the market already accepts is a **30-day money-back guarantee** (SimpleTexting and Salesmsg both run one, as a linked policy page).
4. **Visual language is real product UI, not illustration.** Quo and Salesmsg lead with actual screenshots; SimpleTexting uses example SMS conversations as its main visual device. Loonext should screenshot its real calm stone/petrol inbox and populate threads with trade-specific conversations (a plumber confirming a 2pm water-heater job). No stock photos of smiling call centers.
5. **App-store badges are universal** — every competitor footer has them. Loonext can't show badges; the PWA line ("works on every phone, no download needed") needs to be framed as a *benefit* (nothing to install for the crew) precisely because the badge slot will be conspicuously empty.

### Weaknesses verified live (attack surface)
6. **Podium: zero prices, demo-only, sales phone in the nav.** The anti-Podium page writes itself: "See the price. Pay. Text today. No demo, no phone call."
7. **Textline: tier cards with no dollar amounts** ("contact us to find the best fit"). Quote-game confirmed on the live page.
8. **Heymarket: prices listed but every tier's CTA is "Book a free demo,"** 2-user minimum ($98/mo entry at Standard), **and messages cost extra on top of seats** ($0.03/segment) plus $10/mo/campaign 10DLC fee. Their real all-in number for a 3-person crew sending 500 texts: 3×$49 + $15 + $10 = **$172/mo** vs Loonext Starter $29 all-in.
9. **SimpleTexting's sticker price balloons:** $39 plan + $10/mo number + $4 registration + $20/mo per extra seat + 5.5¢ overages + disclosed-but-buried carrier passthrough. A 3-person team = $79+/mo before overages.
10. **Salesmsg auto-converts the "free" trial to a $49/mo paid plan** and refunds "less message credits used." Trust wedge: Loonext never does dark-pattern conversion.
11. **Quo is per-user:** $15–19/user × a 6-person crew = $90–114/mo vs Loonext Pro $79 for 10 seats. Quo's own /compare pages teach the attack format ("hidden costs" table + switcher testimonials + objection FAQ) — use it on them.
12. **Nobody says anything Canada-first.** SimpleTexting even banners "US & Canada only" apologetically, and no site mentions that Canadian texting works instantly while US 10DLC takes days. Open positioning field for "Canadian businesses: text today, literally."

### Practices worth copying
13. **Quo's fee honesty** ($19.50 one-time TCR registration, $1.50–$3/mo carrier fees, "Cancel anytime — we'll even remind you") is the disclosure bar to beat. Loonext's $29 one-time US registration fee and 3–7-day US timeline should be presented exactly this plainly, on the pricing page and at checkout.
14. **SimpleTexting's compliance reframe** — "SMS marketing is so powerful, it's protected. We help you unlock it" — turns the 10DLC wait into evidence of value. Loonext's honest-timeline section should sell the same way: we handle registration automatically; here's the real wait; Canada is instant.
15. **Industry-page template** (SimpleTexting home-services + Quo HVAC): pain hook → 3–4 trade-specific use cases → example conversation visuals → named case study with a number → how-it-works steps → objection FAQ (compliance, cost, setup time) → cross-links to sibling trades. Quo goes deeper (plumbing, HVAC, cleaning, landscaping, electrician as separate pages) — Loonext should match that granularity for its five ICP trades rather than one generic "home services" page.
16. **Stat-led social proof** ("81% boost in response rate," "57 appointments in 30 days") beats adjective testimonials everywhere it appears. Loonext has none yet — instrument early customers now; until then lead with verifiable product truths (price math, no contract, instant CA texting) instead of manufactured badges.
17. **Free-tool SEO** (Quo's 13 tools, Heymarket's 6 AI generators, SimpleTexting's template library). Cheap, high-intent options for Loonext: missed-call cost calculator for trades, SMS template packs per trade, an "SMS segment counter."
18. **Legal/trust baseline for launch:** Terms, Privacy, AUP, DPA + sub-processors, Security page, Status page (every serious competitor has all six); Textline adds a government-data-request policy and compliance portal as trust theater. A public opt-in/opt-out policy page also directly helps 10DLC campaign approval.
19. **Quo's "Hey AI, learn about us" LLM page** is the first llms.txt-style move in the category — trivially cheap for Loonext to replicate.
20. **Comparison-page inventory to build:** loonext-vs-podium, -vs-quo/openphone, -vs-heymarket, -vs-textline, plus "SimpleTexting alternative." SimpleTexting (9 pages) and Quo (12) prove the pattern; Heymarket has zero and Podium one — they won't be defending those SERPs.
